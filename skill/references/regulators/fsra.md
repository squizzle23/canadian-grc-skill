## IT Risk Management Guidance (GR0016INT, April 1, 2024)

FSRA uses a three-label system for guidance:
- **Interpretation** — FSRA's interpretation of legal requirements. Non-compliance can lead to enforcement.
- **Information** — practices; does not create compliance obligations.
- **Approach** — FSRA's supervisory process; does not create compliance obligations.

The IT Risk Management Guidance is tagged Interpretation + Approach + Information, with different combinations applying per sector.

### Applicability by sector

| Sector | Applicable content |
|---|---|
| Credit Unions and Caisses Populaires | All sectors + Interpretation/Approach specific to CUs (under Sound Business and Financial Practices Rule and Credit Unions and Caisses Populaires Act, 2020) |
| Ontario-Incorporated Insurance Companies and Reciprocals | All sectors + Interpretation/Approach specific to insurers (under Insurance Act) |
| Non-Ontario Incorporated Insurers, Insurance Agents, Agencies, Adjusters, Adjusting Firms | All sectors + Approach specific |
| Loan and Trust Companies | All sectors section only |
| Mortgage Brokerages, Brokers, Agents, Administrators | All sectors + Approach/Information specific (aligns with MBRCC principles) |
| Pension Plan Administrators | All sectors + Interpretation/Approach specific (aligns with CAPSA guideline on cyber risk) |
| Health Service Providers | All sectors section only |
| Credentialing Bodies for Financial Planners/Advisors | All sectors section + sector-specific Approach |

### The Seven Practices for effective IT risk management (Information section — all sectors)

FSRA published seven Practices that form the core IT risk framework for regulated entities. These are not direct legal requirements but are what FSRA expects under its supervisory Approach.

1. **Practice 1: Governance** — The regulated entity or individual has proper governance.
2. **Practice 2: Risk management** — The regulated entity or individual relies on industry-accepted practices to identify and manage IT risks.
3. **Practice 3: Data management** — The regulated entity or individual uses industry-accepted practices to manage data.
4. **Practice 4: Outsourcing** — The regulated entity or individual effectively manages the IT risks associated with outsourcing.
5. **Practice 5: Incident preparedness** — The regulated entity or individual is prepared to respond to IT risk incidents.
6. **Practice 6: Continuity and resiliency** — The regulated entity or individual is prepared to maintain critical business operations during disruption.
7. **Practice 7: Notification of material IT risk incidents** — The regulated entity or individual notifies its regulator(s) in the event of a material IT risk incident.

### Material IT risk incident notification (Practice 7 + Approach section)

**Timeline:** "As soon as is reasonable, which normally falls within **72 hours or sooner**, after determining that an IT risk incident is material."

*Important distinction:* FSRA's 72-hour window is slower than OSFI's 24-hour notification window. Do not conflate the two.

**How to notify:**
- Email the IT Risk Incident Notification Form to `ITriskinbox@fsrao.ca`, OR
- Upload the form and supporting documentation to the Incident Notification Portal, OR
- Direct contact with relationship manager (or Pension Officer/Analyst).

**Reduced burden provision:** FSRA will accept being notified with a comparable form issued by another financial services regulator — useful when OSFI, CIRO, or AMF notifications are also being filed.

### Material IT risk incident — criteria (for all regulated entities EXCEPT pension plan administrators)

Indicators that a material incident has occurred could include, but are not limited to:
- Results in significant operational disruptions to business systems and functions
- Significantly disrupts the consumers'/members' ability to access essential services for a prolonged period
- Impacts a third-party provider to the extent that it has significant impacts on the regulated entity or individual
- Breaches internal risk appetite or thresholds
- Requires non-routine measures or resources
- Results in the exposure of a large amount of confidential data
- Is recurring and could have a significant impact on a cumulative basis
- Is reported to Senior Management or the board of directors
- Is reported to another regulator, a law enforcement agency, the Office of the Privacy Commissioner, etc.
- Results in a claim for cyber insurance

**Materiality determination:** FSRA leaves the determination to the regulated entity based on impact to business, operations, consumers, credit union members, or pension plan beneficiaries.

### Sector-specific Interpretations

#### Credit Unions (under Sound Business and Financial Practices Rule)

FSRA applies all seven Practices as interpretation of the Sound Business and Financial Practices Rule for Ontario CUs. The IT Risk Guidance supplements the separate Operational Risk and Resilience Guidance (CU0088APP).

Each Practice has an Outcomes statement and Approach text specific to CUs:
- Practice 1: Governance — outcomes
- Practice 2: Risk management — outcome
- Practice 3: Data management — outcomes
- Practice 4: Outsourcing — outcomes
- Practice 5: Incident preparedness — outcomes
- Practice 6: Continuity and resilience — outcome
- Practice 7: Notification of material IT risk incidents — outcomes

#### Ontario-Incorporated Insurers and Reciprocals (under Insurance Act)

Same seven Practices interpreted under the Insurance Act. FSRA's supervisory Approach for assessing IT risk management applies.

#### Pension Plan Administrators

Interpretation applies under the Pension Benefits Act. The Guidance is consistent with CAPSA's cyber risk guideline — following FSRA Guidance satisfies CAPSA, and FSRA takes priority in areas of inconsistency.

### Alignment with OSFI direction

FSRA's IT Risk Management Guidance mirrors OSFI B-13 themes but is principles-based and proportionate to FSRA regulated entities (which are generally smaller than OSFI FRFIs). The seven Practices map loosely to B-13 as follows:

| FSRA Practice | Nearest B-13 reference |
|---|---|
| P1 Governance | B-13 Domain 1 (Governance and risk management) |
| P2 Risk management | B-13 Domain 1.3 (Risk management framework) |
| P3 Data management | B-13 Domain 3.1.4 (Data identified, classified, protected) |
| P4 Outsourcing | B-10 (Third-Party Risk Management) |
| P5 Incident preparedness | B-13 Domain 2.7 (Incident and problem management) + Domain 3.4 (Respond) |
| P6 Continuity and resiliency | B-13 Domain 2.9 (DR) + E-21 Section 4.1-4.2 |
| P7 Notification of material IT risk incidents | OSFI Incident Reporting Advisory (but 72h vs. 24h) |

## Operational Risk and Resilience (CU0088APP, March 1, 2024)

Ontario credit-union-specific operational risk and resilience guidance. Applies to Ontario credit unions and caisses populaires under the Credit Unions and Caisses Populaires Act, 2020 and the Sound Business and Financial Practices Rule.

**Guidance type:** Interpretation + Approach + Information.

**Purpose:** Sets out FSRA's interpretation of operational risk and resilience requirements for CUs. Complements the IT Risk Management Guidance — operational risk is broader; IT risk is a subset.

**Relationship to OSFI E-21:** FSRA's CU guidance follows federal direction (E-21) with proportionality for credit union size and complexity. A large Ontario credit union should expect FSRA to apply standards similar to E-21; smaller CUs receive lighter-touch proportionate treatment.

## Output patterns for FSRA questions

**When asked for a FSRA gap analysis:**
1. Identify the sector (CU, insurer, mortgage broker, pension, etc.) — the applicable sections differ.
2. Confirm whether the question is about IT Risk (GR0016INT) or Operational Resilience (CU0088APP for CUs).
3. Cite the relevant Practice (P1–P7) with its Outcome statement.
4. Distinguish Interpretation (compliance obligation) from Information/Approach (guidance, no direct obligation).

**When asked about incident reporting to FSRA:**
1. **72 hours or sooner** after materiality determination — NOT OSFI's 24 hours.
2. Notification to `ITriskinbox@fsrao.ca` or via Incident Notification Portal.
3. Materiality is self-determined by the entity using the criteria list.
4. Comparable forms from other regulators are accepted to reduce duplicate burden.
5. **Privacy overlay:** if personal information is involved, PIPEDA notification to OPC is a separate obligation with its own timeline (as soon as feasible) and 24-month record retention. If Ontario health data is involved, PHIPA notification to the IPC may also apply.

**When asked for CU operational resilience:**
1. Reference CU0088APP for CU operational resilience requirements.
2. Note alignment with federal E-21 but proportional application.
3. The IT Risk Guidance (GR0016INT) is a narrower IT-specific companion.

**Key discovery questions when assessing readiness:**
- FSRA's Practice 7 requires notification of material IT risk incidents within 72 hours. Does the entity have detection capabilities that would surface incidents in time to meet that clock?
- Practice 4 (Outsourcing) holds credit unions accountable for IT risk at their vendors — regardless of contract terms. How is continuous vendor monitoring operationalized?
